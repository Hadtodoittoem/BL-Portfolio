Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

Me and my wife have recently been trying to cook new things, and theres been a few that we liked, and a few that we didn't. I thought it would be fun to make a website that featured some of the things that we were trying or knew that we liked.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

We communicated through Teams. There weren't any problems with me communicating with the lead developer for my page, but he seemed to have some issues with his team.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

Almost. Things that were completed were done fairly well, but some were not, and there was a page that was completely missing. Things could have looked a little prettier, but they still did a pretty good job overall.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

I will definitely look for a team that is enthusiastic and has good communication so they can let me know how things are going or if they need anything from me.

Lead Developer Report

How did you manage your development team and communicate the tasks for your junior devs?

We communicated through teams chat. I assigned my junior developers which pages to create and we collaborated in creating the look of the site.

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?

We also communicated through teams, I asked him a few times for a few images or thoughts on how things should be made.

How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?

It was pretty simple, I published the site through github, and had the link to the site on there. I also shared the link to the website once it was published to the client so they could view it and give any critiques.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?

It was pretty confusing at first. I didn't understand "pulling" so I messed up what some of my devs had made. I got the hang of it more or less by the end.

General Experience and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

Most challenging part was working with other people that didn't do any work.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

I enjoyed building different pages depending on what the client needed. It was fun.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

I'd try to get in contact with my team way earlier and try to help them progress so I wasn't stuck waiting on them.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

Yes, I feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience. I learned how to communicate effectively with team members and clients, and how to manage my time and tasks efficiently for the most part.
